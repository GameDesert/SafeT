Web Safety

Traces

When you visit a website, such as this one, there are several ways in which it will leave a trace on your device. Through these, someone can later see that you have visited that website.

The most common traces left by websites are through:

  • Cookies
    When you visit a majority of websites on the internet, they will store a small piece of information on your device called a cookie. This can be used by other people who use your device to see if you have visited a website before.

  • Browsing History
    Every time you visit a website, your browser will record this in its browsing history. Somebody could look at this list and see all the websites you have visited.

  • Downloads
    When you download a file from a website, it will—naturally—remain on your device until you delete it.

  • Spyware and Monitoring Software
    There exist special computer programmes that keep a log of everything a user does on a device. These can be very hard to circumvent, and we recommend using a different trusted device if you suspect yours has this type of software on it.

Covering Your Tracks

If you are using a shared device and/or are vulnerable or in a dangerous situation, it may be necessary for you to hide the fact that you have visited this website.

Quick Exit

If you need to exit this website quickly, you can click the triangle in the bottom right corner of the screen (◢) or press the esc key on your keyboard (usually located in the top left). This will take you to a safe, innocuous page and clear any cookies that this website has saved. However, this page is unable to clear your browsing history for you, so you will need to do this yourself.

Private Browsing

The best method to cover your tracks is to use private browsing:

  • "Incognito" on Google Chrome
    Ctrl + Shift + N or ⌘ + Shift + N

  • "Private Window" on Safari
    ⌘ + Shift + N

  • "InPrivate" on Edge and Internet Explorer
    Ctrl + Shift + N or ⌘ + Shift + N

  • "Private Browsing" on Mozilla Firefox
    Ctrl + Shift + P or ⌘ + Shift + P

  • "Private Browsing" on Opera
    Ctrl + Shift + N or ⌘ + Shift + N

This will prevent websites from storing cookies or remaining in your browsing history. However, device administrators will still be able to see what you search and visit.

Clearing Cookies and History

If you visit a site and it leaves cookies or a trace in your browsing history, you can easily remove these. Note, however, that a recently cleared browsing history may cause some people to be suspicious, it is therefore recommended that you only clear traces from the specific website you don't want found.

Alternative Devices

If your devices at home are not safe to use as someone else has access to them, consider visiting a library to browse the web or ask a trusted friend to borrow their device.
