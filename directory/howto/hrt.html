HRT

This is not medical advice. It is not written by a medical professional. You must fully research any pertinent information and speak to a licensed medical professional before taking any medical decisions, undergoing a procedure, or taking a new medication.

This resource is written for those aged 18 and above, who have the legal capacity to make fully independent decisions about their health.

Advice for those below the age of 18 can be found on the "Advice for Parents and Guardians" section of this site, though we do still recommend reading this guide as there are relevant details.

Advice for Parents and Guardians

Get a Prescription

To get a prescription, you need to get a diagnosis of gender dysphoria or a related condition.

Read our guide on getting diagnosed

Once you have a diagnosis, your healthcare provider (either public or private) should issue you a prescription that you can fill at your local pharmacy; it's best to call ahead as they may not have the medication on-hand.

Take Your Prescription

The instructions on how to administer your prescription will be given to you by your healthcare provider, and should also be listed on the prescription.

If your prescription is on the NHS, your GP or other NHS care provider will ask you to come in for liver function tests. If your prescription is private, you can approach your GP to discuss organising these tests, but they may refuse to help you.

In this case, the Chelsea and Westminster Hospital NHS Foundation Trust operates a sexual health clinic—56 Dean Street—which also offers services specific to trans and non-binary people. This includes liver function tests and hormone injections (once prescribed by a UK registered medical doctor). Appointments must be booked online or over the phone. Src: NHS
